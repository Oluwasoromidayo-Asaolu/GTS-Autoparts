How to Use Accessibility Features on Your Device

Browsing with Accessibility Features on a Desktop Computer

  • Use the screen magnifier and large font support: Use the built-in features in your browser to zoom in and out. Press Ctrl/Cmd + + to zoom in, Ctrl/Cmd + - to zoom out, and Ctrl/Cmd + 0 to reset to the default zoom.
  • Customize your reading experience: On your internet browser window, activate the reader view from the address bar. Reader view is compatible with browsers like Firefox, Safari, Opera, and Edge. You can also press F9 or Ctrl + Shift + R to enter reader mode.
  • Keyboard navigation: Use the Tab and Arrow keys on the keyboard to navigate through the page and to access specific links.
  • Screen Reader Tips:
    • When you're ready to perform an action like adding an item to your cart, use your screen reader's “Button” hotkey to navigate to the action button.
    • If you get stuck in a page section that continuously loads images, use your screen reader’s heading keys to navigate to a heading outside that section.
    • Product search result titles are structured as headings. Use the heading keys or heading dialog to navigate your search results.

Mobile Accessibility Features

Mobile accessibility features are designed for users with visual, hearing, or mobility impairments to make mobile devices easier to use.

For Android Devices:

  • Open the Settings application on your device.
  • Navigate to the "Accessibility" section, then select "Accessibility Menu".
  • Turn on the "Accessibility Menu Shortcut" option.
  • Confirm the action by selecting "OK".

For iOS Devices:

  • Open the Settings app on your device.
  • Go to "Settings" > "Accessibility".
  • Select the accessibility features you wish to enable from the available options (vision, physical and motor, hearing, and general accessibility).
